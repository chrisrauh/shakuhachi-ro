I'm Christian, a shakuhachi student. This is a place to share and discover shakuhachi scores — think of it like [The Sessions](https://thesession.org/), but for shakuhachi.

## Forking

If you've used GitHub or similar tools, forking will be familiar. If not: any score here can be forked, which gives you your own editable copy. Handy when you want to annotate, transpose, or adapt a piece. Your version is independent; you won't affect the original.

## The web component

A big part of why I built this is the renderer — a standalone library for displaying shakuhachi notation on any webpage. This live example shows it in action:

It's designed so anyone can embed scores in a blog post, a comment, or their own tool. More in the [project source on GitHub](https://github.com/chrisrauh/shakuhachi-ro).

## On AI

To be honest: this site wouldn't exist without it. I'd had the idea for years but never enough time. Claude Code let me turn the pockets of time I have into something real.

I understand that's not neutral ground for everyone. The technology raises genuine questions and I don't dismiss them. I just think it's worth being upfront about how this was made.
